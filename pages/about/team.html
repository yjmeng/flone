Naomi Mwaura

Founder & Executive Director

Naomi has over 7 years experience in project management and community development. She currently works as a communications Associate at the Institute for Transportation and Development Policy. She is the Founder of Flone Initiative and co-founder of Udada Festival Trust, an organization that highlights and celebrates women in the arts. Naomi holds a Bachelors Degree in Psychology (Honors). As part of Mandela Washington Fellowship for Young African Leaders, Naomi completed a civic leadership fellowship at Tulane University, USA.

Holly Kearl

Board Member

Holly Kearl is the founder of Stop Street Harassment and the author of several books and studies on gender-based street harassment. The most recent study, released in Feb. 2018, tracks the numbers behind the #MeToo Movement in the USA. She’s penned more than 85 op-eds on sexual harassment issues for outlets like NYT, Washington Post, Guardian and CNN and is a renowned speaker globally. She’s also done consulting work on these topics for UN Women, US Dept. of State, Lyft, Runner’s World and various local government bodies. Currently, she works as a community manager for the Aspen Institute's New Voices Fellowship in the Global Innovators department. Holly has a master’s degree in public policy and women and gender studies from George Washington University.

John Mark

Board Member

John is the Project Coordinator for Amalgamated Transport and General Workers Union (ATGWU) in Uganda. ATWGU has a women’s committee that support mapping and organizing of women in informal transport. He is the author of several publications on issues related to transport workers and union organizing. He was recently involved in conducting a Preliminary Labour Impact Assessment for the Bus Rapid Transit (BRT) implementation for Nairobi, and designed and successfully implemented strategies for organizing informal transport workers in Uganda, Kenya and Tanzania under the International Transport Workers’ Informal Workers’ Project.

Elizabeth Njoki Kinuthia

Board Member

Elizabeth has extensive experience in the Kenyan public transport industry. Before joing the matatu industry, she worked as a long distance truck conductor for 2 years.
